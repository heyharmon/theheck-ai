# Cluster Expansion Framework

This document provides a systematic methodology for expanding pillar keywords into comprehensive keyword clusters.

## The Modifier Matrix

Use these modifier categories to systematically generate cluster keywords from each pillar:

### 1. Question Modifiers

Questions represent high-intent informational searches. Apply these to every pillar:

| Modifier | Example (pillar: "email marketing") |
|----------|-------------------------------------|
| what is | what is email marketing |
| how to | how to do email marketing |
| how does | how does email marketing work |
| why | why email marketing is important |
| when to | when to send marketing emails |
| where to | where to learn email marketing |
| who | who needs email marketing |
| which | which email marketing platform |

### 2. Comparison Modifiers

Comparison searches indicate commercial investigation intent:

| Modifier | Example |
|----------|---------|
| vs | email marketing vs social media marketing |
| or | Mailchimp or ConvertKit |
| alternatives | email marketing alternatives |
| compared to | email marketing compared to SMS |
| difference between | difference between email marketing and automation |

### 3. Qualifier Modifiers

Qualifiers narrow the audience or specify attributes:

| Modifier Type | Examples |
|---------------|----------|
| Quality | best, top, leading, professional |
| Cost | free, cheap, affordable, budget, enterprise, premium |
| Audience | for small business, for beginners, for startups, for ecommerce |
| Time | 2024, latest, new, modern |
| Size | small, large, enterprise, SMB |

### 4. Intent Modifiers

These signal where the searcher is in the buying journey:

| Modifier | Intent Signal | Example |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| examples | Early research | email marketing examples |
| templates | Ready to implement | email marketing templates |
| tools | Evaluating solutions | email marketing tools |
| software | Evaluating solutions | email marketing software |
| services | Ready to buy | email marketing services |
| agency | Ready to outsource | email marketing agency |
| pricing | Close to purchase | email marketing pricing |
| demo | Close to purchase | email marketing demo |
| free trial | Close to purchase | email marketing free trial |

### 5. Specificity Modifiers

Long-tail variations for specific use cases:

| Modifier Type | Examples |
|---------------|----------|
| Industry vertical | email marketing for restaurants, B2B email marketing |
| Use case | email marketing for lead generation, email marketing for retention |
| Feature-specific | email marketing automation, email marketing segmentation |
| Platform-specific | email marketing for Shopify, email marketing with WordPress |
| Format-specific | email marketing newsletter, drip email campaigns |

## Expansion Process

For each pillar keyword, follow this process:

### Step 1: Generate the Core Questions

Apply all question modifiers to get 7-10 question-based clusters.

### Step 2: Identify Key Comparisons

Determine what the pillar is commonly compared against:
- Direct competitors/alternatives
- Adjacent solutions
- Old vs new approaches

### Step 3: Apply Relevant Qualifiers

Not all qualifiers apply to every pillar. Select those that match the target audience:
- B2B audience → enterprise, professional, business
- Budget-conscious → free, affordable, cheap
- Quality-focused → best, top, leading

### Step 4: Map the Buyer Journey

Ensure clusters cover all stages:
- Awareness: "what is", "why", "benefits"
- Consideration: "vs", "alternatives", "how to choose"
- Decision: "pricing", "reviews", "demo"

### Step 5: Add Specificity Layers

Based on the business's target audience, add:
- Industry-specific variations
- Use-case specific variations
- Feature-specific variations

## Web Search Validation

Use web_search and web_fetch to validate and expand clusters:

### Search the Pillar Term
Look for:
- What subtopics appear in search results → Consider as clusters
- What content formats rank (guides, comparisons, tools)
- What questions the top-ranking content answers

### Analyze Competitor Content
1. Search the pillar keyword to see which competitors rank
2. Use web_fetch to visit competitor pages that rank well
3. Analyze their content structure:
   - What topics do they cover?
   - What subheadings do they use?
   - What related topics do they link to?

### Check for Gaps
Ask: "What topics would a comprehensive resource on [pillar] include that competitors miss or cover poorly?"

## Cluster Depth Guidelines

**Focus on the 80/20 rule:** Identify the clusters that will drive the most impact, not an arbitrary count. Quality beats quantity.

| Pillar Type | Guidance | Focus |
|-------------|----------|-------|
| Core offering pillar | Deep coverage | Prioritize high-intent clusters that directly support sales |
| Problem/audience pillar | Moderate coverage | Focus on clusters that capture demand at awareness stage |
| Industry/category pillar | Selective coverage | Choose clusters where you can differentiate |
| Geographic pillar | Focused coverage | Only clusters with clear local relevance |

**Selection criteria for clusters:**
- Does this cluster represent a real search intent?
- Can the business create genuinely valuable content for this topic?
- Will ranking for this cluster move the business closer to its goals?

Don't add clusters just to hit a number. Each cluster should earn its place.

## Example Cluster Expansion

**Pillar: "Project Management Software"**

| Category | Cluster Keywords |
|----------|------------------|
| Questions | what is project management software, how to choose project management software, why use project management software |
| Comparisons | Asana vs Monday, project management software alternatives, project management vs task management |
| Qualifiers | best project management software, free project management software, project management software for small teams |
| Intent | project management software features, project management software pricing, project management software reviews |
| Specificity | project management software for agencies, project management software with time tracking, agile project management software |

## Avoiding Cluster Overlap

When expanding multiple pillars, watch for overlap:

1. **Assign ownership** — Each cluster belongs to one pillar
2. **Check for duplicates** — Before adding a cluster, verify it's not already under another pillar
3. **Consolidate related terms** — "email marketing tools" and "email marketing software" may be the same cluster

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing clusters, verify:

- [ ] Every cluster has clear search intent
- [ ] Clusters don't significantly overlap
- [ ] The business can credibly create content for each cluster
- [ ] Clusters cover the full buyer journey
- [ ] No obvious gaps in topic coverage
