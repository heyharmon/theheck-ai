# Search Intent Mapping

This document provides guidelines for classifying search intent and mapping keywords to appropriate content types.

## The Four Search Intents

### 1. Informational Intent

**What it means:** The searcher wants to learn something or find an answer to a question.

**Signal words:**
- Question words: what, how, why, when, where, who
- Learning words: guide, tutorial, learn, understand, examples
- Explanation words: definition, meaning, explained

**Content types:**
- Blog posts / Articles
- Guides / Tutorials
- How-to content
- Explainer videos
- FAQ pages
- Glossary entries

**Conversion potential:** Low-medium (top of funnel, builds awareness and trust)

**Example keywords:**
- "what is content marketing"
- "how to write a business plan"
- "email marketing best practices"

---

### 2. Navigational Intent

**What it means:** The searcher is looking for a specific website, page, or brand.

**Signal words:**
- Brand names
- Product names
- "login", "sign in", "website"
- Specific page types: "pricing page", "contact"

**Content types:**
- Homepage
- Product pages
- About page
- Login/account pages
- Contact page

**Conversion potential:** Medium-high (if searching for your brand)

**Example keywords:**
- "Slack login"
- "HubSpot pricing"
- "Nike official website"

**Note:** Navigational intent for competitor brands represents an opportunity—create comparison content to capture some of this traffic.

---

### 3. Commercial Investigation Intent

**What it means:** The searcher is researching options before making a purchase decision.

**Signal words:**
- Comparison words: vs, versus, compare, comparison, difference
- Evaluation words: best, top, review, reviews, alternatives
- Feature words: features, pros and cons, benefits

**Content types:**
- Comparison pages ([A] vs [B])
- Listicles (Best [X] for [Y])
- Review content
- Buyer's guides
- Feature breakdowns
- Alternative pages

**Conversion potential:** High (middle-bottom of funnel)

**Example keywords:**
- "Asana vs Monday"
- "best CRM for small business"
- "Mailchimp alternatives"
- "project management software reviews"

---

### 4. Transactional Intent

**What it means:** The searcher is ready to take action—buy, sign up, download, or convert.

**Signal words:**
- Action words: buy, purchase, order, sign up, subscribe, download
- Deal words: discount, coupon, deal, sale, pricing, cost
- Ready words: near me, free trial, demo, quote

**Content types:**
- Landing pages
- Product pages
- Pricing pages
- Free trial / Demo pages
- Contact / Quote request pages
- Checkout pages

**Conversion potential:** Highest (bottom of funnel)

**Example keywords:**
- "buy project management software"
- "CRM free trial"
- "marketing agency near me"
- "email marketing pricing"

---

## Intent Classification Matrix

Use this matrix to quickly classify keywords:

| If the keyword contains... | Likely intent |
|---------------------------|---------------|
| what, how, why, guide, tutorial | Informational |
| [brand name], login, official | Navigational |
| best, vs, compare, review, alternative | Commercial Investigation |
| buy, pricing, free trial, demo, near me | Transactional |

## Content Type Recommendations

### For Informational Keywords

| Keyword Pattern | Recommended Content |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| "what is [X]" | Comprehensive explainer article (1500-2500 words) |
| "how to [X]" | Step-by-step tutorial with visuals |
| "[X] guide" | Long-form pillar guide (3000+ words) |
| "[X] examples" | Listicle with real examples and analysis |
| "[X] best practices" | Expert-level article with actionable tips |
| "[X] tips" | Scannable listicle format |

### For Commercial Investigation Keywords

| Keyword Pattern | Recommended Content |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| "[A] vs [B]" | Direct comparison page with feature table |
| "best [X]" | Ranked listicle with criteria explanation |
| "[X] alternatives" | Alternative options page with pros/cons |
| "[X] reviews" | Aggregated review content or original review |
| "[X] for [audience]" | Audience-specific recommendation page |

### For Transactional Keywords

| Keyword Pattern | Recommended Content |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| "[X] pricing" | Pricing page or pricing comparison content |
| "buy [X]" | Product page with clear CTA |
| "[X] free trial" | Dedicated trial landing page |
| "[X] demo" | Demo request page with form |
| "[X] near me" | Location pages or local landing pages |

## Mixed Intent Keywords

Some keywords have ambiguous intent. Handle these by:

1. **Check the SERP** — Search the keyword and see what types of content rank
2. **Cover multiple intents** — Create content that addresses both informational and commercial aspects
3. **Use clear navigation** — Include CTAs for users ready to convert while providing information for those still researching

**Example:** "email marketing software"
- Could be informational (learning about the category)
- Could be commercial investigation (comparing options)
- Could be transactional (ready to buy)

**Solution:** Create a comprehensive guide that explains the category, compares top options, and includes clear CTAs for those ready to try.

| Intent | Typical Word Count | Key Elements |
|--------|-------------------|--------------|
| Informational | 1500-3000+ | Comprehensive coverage, examples, visuals |
| Navigational | 500-1500 | Clear information, easy navigation |
| Commercial Investigation | 2000-4000 | Feature comparisons, pros/cons, recommendations |
