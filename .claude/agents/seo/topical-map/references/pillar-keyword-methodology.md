# Pillar Keyword Methodology

This document provides detailed guidance for identifying and validating pillar keywords.

## What Makes a Good Pillar Keyword

A pillar keyword should meet the following criteria:

### 1. Broad Enough to Support Clusters

The keyword must have sufficient depth to generate meaningful related subtopics. Test this by asking: "Can I write multiple distinct, high-value articles about this topic?"

**Good pillar examples:**
- "email marketing" → supports clusters like automation, templates, best practices, tools, metrics, etc.
- "project management software" → supports clusters like features, comparisons, use cases, integrations, etc.

**Too narrow (better as clusters):**
- "email subject line tips" → this is a single article topic
- "Gantt chart templates" → this is a subtopic of project management

### 2. Aligned with Business Offerings

The pillar should directly relate to what the business sells or the problems it solves. Every pillar should answer: "Why would ranking for this help us get customers?"

### 3. Appropriate Search Intent

Pillar keywords typically have mixed intent (informational + commercial). Pure navigational terms (brand names) or pure transactional terms ("buy X") are usually not good pillars.

### 4. Achievable Competition Level

For new businesses with no existing presence, avoid pillars dominated by massive authority sites unless there's a clear angle (geographic, niche, or format differentiation).

## Pillar Identification Framework

### Step A: List All Offerings

Start by listing every product, service, or tool the business offers. Each offering is a potential pillar candidate.

**Example for a CRM company:**
- Contact management
- Sales pipeline
- Email integration
- Reporting/analytics

### Step B: Identify Audience Problems

What problems does the target audience have that lead them to need this business? These problem-framed pillars often have high informational intent and strong conversion potential.

**Example problems for a CRM company:**
- "how to organize customer data"
- "sales team productivity"
- "customer retention strategies"

### Step C: Map Industry Categories

What broader industry or category does the business belong to? These pillars build topical authority even if they're more competitive.

**Example for a CRM company:**
- "CRM software"
- "sales software"
- "customer relationship management"

### Step D: Consider Geographic Modifiers

If the business serves a specific geography, geo-modified pillars may be appropriate:

- "[Service] + [City]" — "web design Salt Lake City"
- "[Service] + [Region]" — "HVAC services Utah"
- "[Service] + [Country]" — "accounting software UK"

Geographic pillars are most relevant for:
- Local service businesses
- Businesses with regional focus
- Products with country-specific features or compliance

## Pillar Validation Checklist

Before finalizing a pillar keyword, validate it with web_search:

| Check | How to Validate | Pass Criteria |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|
| Depth | Search the term and review what subtopics appear in results | Multiple distinct subtopics visible in search results |
| Intent match | Review the top 10 results | Results match content the business can create |
| Competition | Review who ranks on page 1 | At least some non-giant sites ranking |
| Competitor presence | Search "[competitor name] [pillar term]" | Competitors have content on this topic |

**Validation process:**
1. Search the pillar keyword directly
2. Search "[competitor] [pillar keyword]" to see if competitors target it
3. If a competitor ranks, use web_fetch to analyze their page content

## Pillar Patterns by Business Type

The business type should drive pillar selection strategy. Start by identifying the business type, then apply the relevant patterns.

### SaaS / Software
**Characteristics:** Recurring revenue, feature-driven differentiation, often global reach

**Primary pillar patterns:**
- [Product category] software — "project management software"
- [Problem] solution — "team collaboration solution"
- [Use case] tools — "remote work tools"

**Secondary patterns:**
- [Industry] + [product category] — "legal practice management software"
- [Audience] + [product category] — "project management for startups"

**Focus:** Product category and problem pillars typically drive highest intent traffic.

---

### Service Business (Local)
**Characteristics:** Geographic constraints, relationship-driven, capacity-limited

**Primary pillar patterns:**
- [Service] + [location] — "web design Salt Lake City"
- [Service type] — "custom web development"
- [Problem] services — "website redesign services"

**Secondary patterns:**
- [Industry] + [service] — "healthcare website design"

**Focus:** Geographic pillars are essential. Prioritize service + location combinations for your service area.

---

### Service Business (National/Remote)
**Characteristics:** Serves clients remotely, not geographically constrained

**Primary pillar patterns:**
- [Service type] — "SEO consulting"
- [Problem] services — "lead generation services"
- [Industry] consulting/agency — "SaaS marketing agency"

**Secondary patterns:**
- [Service] for [audience] — "SEO for startups"

**Focus:** Problem and industry-specific pillars often outperform generic service terms.

---

### E-commerce / Products
**Characteristics:** Physical or digital products, inventory, shipping considerations

**Primary pillar patterns:**
- [Product category] — "standing desks"
- Best [product type] — "best ergonomic keyboards"
- [Product type] for [audience] — "office chairs for programmers"

**Secondary patterns:**
- [Material/feature] + [product] — "bamboo standing desks"
- [Brand] + [product type] — Only if you're the brand

**Focus:** Product category pillars with qualifier modifiers (best, for [audience]) capture comparison shopping intent.

---

### Content / Media / Creator
**Characteristics:** Monetizes attention, builds audience, often ad or sponsorship revenue

**Primary pillar patterns:**
- [Topic] guide — "productivity guide"
- How to [topic] — "how to start a podcast"
- [Topic] resources — "freelancer resources"

**Secondary patterns:**
- [Industry] news — Only if publishing frequently
- [Topic] for [audience] — "investing for beginners"

**Focus:** Informational pillars dominate. Choose topics where you can publish consistently and build authority.

---

### Agency / Consultancy
**Characteristics:** Expertise-driven, project-based, often serves specific industries

**Primary pillar patterns:**
- [Service] agency — "content marketing agency"
- [Industry] + [service] — "fintech marketing"
- [Service] consulting — "brand strategy consulting"

**Secondary patterns:**
- [Service] for [company size] — "marketing for startups"
- [Problem] + consulting — "growth consulting"

**Focus:** Industry-specific pillars often convert better than generic service pillars. Demonstrate expertise in a vertical.

## Number of Pillars

Focus on the highest-impact pillars rather than hitting a specific count. For most businesses with 1-6 offerings:

| Business Size | Typical Range | Guidance |
|---------------|---------------|----------|
| 1 offering | 2-4 pillars | Offering + key problem/category pillars |
| 2-3 offerings | 3-5 pillars | Core offerings + supporting pillars |
| 4-6 offerings | 4-6 pillars | May not need a pillar per offering—group related offerings |

**Quality over quantity:** It's better to dominate 3 pillars than spread thin across 8. Choose pillars where the business can realistically become an authority.
