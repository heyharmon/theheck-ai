## Design Context

### Users
Broad audience spanning developers, founders, technical non-developers, non-technical people, and generally curious people exploring the AI agent ecosystem. They arrive wanting to understand what tools exist, how they differ, and which ones are relevant to their needs. Many are overwhelmed by the pace of new releases and need clarity, not more noise.

### Brand Personality
Credible. The site earns trust through clear, opinionated descriptions that tell you what a tool is, who it's for, and what it's not for. It earns excitement by making a fast-moving ecosystem feel navigable and discoverable.

### Emotional Goals
- **Excitement**: "Finally, all of these tools are organized in a way I can actually explore." The feeling of discovering capabilities you didn't know existed.
- **Trust**: "These descriptions are honest and useful — they actually help me understand what this does." No marketing fluff, no hype inflation.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **References**: Product Hunt (discovery energy, clean cards), Vercel (typographic confidence, developer credibility, restrained palette), Anthropic (intellectual clarity, generous whitespace, trust through simplicity)
- **Anti-references**: Generic AI directories with gradient-heavy hero sections, particle backgrounds, "powered by AI" badges, and indistinguishable card grids. No AI slop aesthetic.
- **Theme**: Light mode. Dark hero section for contrast. Clean, information-dense without feeling cluttered. The design should feel like a well-organized reference, not a marketing site.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over flash** — Every design choice should make information easier to parse. If a visual element doesn't help the user understand or navigate, remove it.
2. **Density with breathing room** — Show a lot of information without overwhelming. Use whitespace strategically, not decoratively.
3. **Credible, not corporate** — The tone is expert and direct, like a well-maintained wiki or a senior engineer's recommendation list. Not sterile enterprise, not startup hype.
4. **Distinct from AI slop** — No gratuitous gradients, no animated mesh backgrounds, no generic "futuristic" aesthetic. Earn visual interest through typography, layout, and content quality.
5. **Discovery-first** — The interface should reward browsing. Filters, comparisons, and project pages should feel like exploring, not searching a database.

### Technical Design System
- **Fonts**: Inter (body/headings), Space Grotesk (display), JetBrains Mono (code/stats)
- **Palette**: Neutral grays (neutral-50 through neutral-950) + indigo accent (indigo-600 primary). Emerald for positive/success, amber for warnings, rose for alerts.
- **Spacing**: Section padding py-24/sm:py-32, container max-w-5xl, card gap gap-6
- **Components**: Cards with neutral-200 borders, rounded-xl, hover:shadow-md transitions. Ghost and primary button variants.
- **Dark hero**: bg-neutral-950 with white/neutral-400 text for homepage hero contrast.
